
== CRQC evolution

"Thus, a successful public demonstration of Shor’s algorithm on a 32-bit elliptic curve should not be seen as a wake-up call to adopt PQC as much as a potential signal that PQC adoption has already failed."
    - pg 11

The logic is a warning about false reassurance from early milestones.

The concern is this: people might assume the threat timeline looks like:

-----
32-bit demo → 64-bit demo → 128-bit demo → 256-bit demo → now migrate
-----

...meaning a public 32-bit demonstration gives you years of warning before the real threat arrives.

The paper argues this assumption is wrong for two reasons:

. Progress is not gradual — it's threshold-based
+
Quantum computing advances in discontinuous jumps when engineering barriers are overcome (error correction thresholds, coherent interconnects, etc.).
Once a team solves the fundamental scaling problems to reach 32-bit ECDLP, there may be very little standing between them and 256-bit.
The intermediate milestones (64, 128-bit) may fall quickly in succession, or be skipped entirely by teams with more resources.

. The dangerous actor won't announce themselves
+
A nation-state or well-funded adversary building a CRQC has every incentive to not publish a 32-bit demonstration.
They would work in secret and go straight for 256-bit.
So the only actors who would publicly demonstrate 32-bit capability are researchers or less sophisticated actors — who are precisely the ones who aren't the primary threat.

Putting it together::

If you see a public 32-bit demo, one of two things is true:

* Migration is underway and will complete in time — good
* Migration hasn't seriously started yet — in which case the 32-bit demo signals that the window to migrate has likely already closed, because the serious actors are already further along privately.

The demo isn't the starting gun. It may be the finish line.
